\subsection*{Inverse Trig Derivatives}

Derivatives of the inverse trigonometric functions are shown below.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccc}
$\disp\frac{d}{dx}\big(\arcsin(x)\big)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$ &  & $\disp\frac{d}{dx}\big(\arccos(x)\big)=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$ \\
& \hspace{10mm} & \\
$\disp\frac{d}{dx}\big(\arctan(x)\big)=\frac{1}{1+x^2}$ & & $\disp\frac{d}{dx}\big(\arccot(x)\big)=\frac{-1}{1+x^2}$ \\
& \hspace{10mm} & \\

$\disp\frac{d}{dx}\big(\arcsec(x)\big)=\frac{1}{\vert x \vert \sqrt{x^2-1}}$ & & $\disp\frac{d}{dx}\big(\arccsc(x)\big)=\frac{-1}{\vert x \vert \sqrt{x^2-1}}$ \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\vspace{3mm}

\Example Prove that $\disp\frac{d}{dx}\big(\arctan(x)\big)=\frac{1}{x^2}$.

\paragraph{Reminders.} Be mindful of recognizing when to apply the \textit{chain rule}.

$$\frac{d}{dx}\Big(\arcsin\big(f(x)\big)\Big)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\big(f(x)\big)^2}}\cdot f'(x)$$

\vspace{3mm}

Also, be aware of equivalent notations for \textit{inverse trig functions}.

$$\arccos(x)=\cos^{-1}(x)$$
